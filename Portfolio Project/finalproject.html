Coronavirus is leaving its mark on the NBA

by Travis Chase April 23rd, 2020

Coronavirus is leaving its mark on the NBA

Creator:Eric Risberg/Credit:AP

The effect of the COVID-19 virus on economies and businesses across the world has been unprecedented. From families to countries around the world, precautionary and quarantine methods have been taken to reduce the spread of the virus. Likely one of the most unexpected outcomes of the virus: the shutdown of the billion-dollar NBA industry.

On March 11th, Rudy Gobert of the Utah Jazz tested positive for the coronavirus, which led the NBA to halt its season for the foreseeable future. Prior to the diagnosis,Gobert had been joking around with members of the media by touching objects in the media room. At the time,“Gobert didn’t realize he had contracted the virus”, which put many other people at risk.

In a video posted by Gobert on his twitter account, he apologized for being careless and urged everyone to take the virus seriously. In the video, Gobert said,“It's all about protecting yourself and the people around you. I wish I would have taken this thing more seriously and I hope everyone else will do so because we can do it together. Take care and stay safe.”

The decision to put games on hold came as a shock to many players and staff employed in the league. Any games after March 12th would either be postponed or cancelled. The league was faced with a decision of whether to push the season back, cancel some games, or cancel the rest of the season entirely. This decision left many NBA players in shock.

A day after Gobert tested positive, Donovan Mitchell of the Utah Jazz, also tested positive for the coronavirus. The diagnosis of the two players “was” problematic, but the issue was that neither player showed symptoms. This prevented the league from acting urgently to slow the spread of the virus.

On March 14th, Detroit Pistons forward, Christian Wood tested positive for the coronavirus. A few days later on March 17th, it was announced that Forward, Kevin Durant and three other Brooklyn Nets players had tested positive for the coronavirus. Only one of the four players showed symptoms of the virus.

On March 19th,between players and staff on four different teams, seven different people tested positive for Covid-19. These teams include the Lakers, Nuggets, Sixers and Celtics. Most recently on March 28th, New York Knicks owner, James Dolan, tested positive for the coronavirus.

These cases are even more reason to close all NBA facilities. However, many people have posed the question, how much money could the NBA lose it if it doesn’t continue its season? The predictions are over a billion dollars. According to WVEC, an ABC-affiliated television station, the NBA had earned $8 billion in revenue in 2018, but due to the coronavirus, the NBA is expected to lose more than $1 billion. Commissioner Adam Silver said the league would close its doors for at least a month. The NBA is still working its way to fill a viable solution to continue the 2019-2020 season after the hiatus and rumors of potential revenue loss.

Creator:Justen Beers/Credit:Beers and Associates Real Estate

NBA owners have most recently lobbied for the NBA season to continue during the summer despite the 2020-2021 season start date being so close. The potential site for the end of this season, according to a CNBC report, is Las Vegas at the Thomas and Mack Center. It would be at a smaller venue and would be played there without fans.

As for whether the NBA would finish the regular season or start from the playoffs is up in the air. One thing is for certain though, the Coronavirus has put the NBA and the rest of the world on hold.
